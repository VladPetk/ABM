# Model Blindspots Register

This document registers the structural and empirical **blindspots** identified in the `polarlab` / `"Calm to Camps"` Agent-Based Model (ABM) of US political polarization (1980–2025). These blindspots represent areas where mathematically convenient model rules conflict with or fail to represent established peer-reviewed social science literature.

---

## 1. The Media/Internet Paradox (Age-Gradient Mismatch)
*   **The Model Rule:** Bounded confidence influence, selective exposure, and media consumption (e.g., online media-diet algorithms) are modeled as primary drivers of partisan sorting and affective polarization.
*   **The Empirical Finding:** 
    *   US affective polarization grew fastest among older demographics (65+) who are the *least* likely to use the internet or social media (*Boxell, Gentzkow & Shapiro 2017 PNAS*).
    *   Replacing algorithmic social media feeds with chronological feeds changes exposure but has **zero** detectable short-term effect on affective polarization or ideological extremity (*Guess et al. 2023 Science*).
*   **The Blindspot:** A model that relies heavily on internet exposure, social media algorithms, or online echo chambers as drivers will fail to reproduce the observed age gradient (where the old polarized fastest). The model over-represents the causal weight of online media curation.

## 2. Meta-Perception Decoupling (Intervention Durability decay)
*   **The Model Rule:** Correcting agents' misperceptions of the out-party's positions or animus reduces downstream affective polarization.
*   **The Empirical Finding:** 
    *   While informational corrections reliably update *metaperceptions* and reduce support for violence (*Mernyk et al. 2022 PNAS*), their downstream impact on partisan animosity and general affective polarization is small, highly inconsistent, and decays rapidly—frequently showing null results in longitudinal follow-ups (*Voelkel et al. 2024 Science Megastudy*; *Dias et al. 2024 PNAS Nexus*).
*   **The Blindspot:** Modeling informational corrections as a durable or dominant depolarizing force is structurally ungrounded. In reality, correcting out-party beliefs does not translate into stable improvements in affective warmth.

## 3. Mass-Elite Feedback (The Extremist Penalty & Leapfrogging)
*   **The Model Rule:** Candidates and elites shift positions smoothly to track constituent medians or party centers (Hotelling-Downs spatial representation).
*   **The Empirical Finding:** 
    *   Nominating an extremist candidate carries a severe general election penalty of 9–13 percentage points in vote share (*Hall 2015 APSR*).
    *   Representation is characterized by "leapfrogging," where elite members of Congress are systematically more extreme than their constituents, and party flips cause a seat's representative to jump from one ideological extreme to the other (*Bafumi & Herron 2010 APSR*).
*   **The Blindspot:** A spatial candidate selection mechanism that tracks median constituents without modeling the severe electoral cost of extremism or candidate self-selection (moderates opting out of running, *Thomsen 2017*) represents a major structural blindspot.

## 4. Identity Directionality (The Politics-to-Identity Reverse Flow)
*   **The Model Rule:** Social identities (e.g., religious affiliation, ethnicity, lifestyle sorting) are treated as static independent variables that drive political sorting.
*   **The Empirical Finding:** 
    *   Americans change their religious identification, ethnic identity, and class alignment in panel data over time to fit their prior partisan/ideological alignments (*Egan 2020 AJPS*; *Margolis 2018*).
*   **The Blindspot:** Treating social sorting as unidirectional (identity → politics) ignores the reverse-causal feedback loop where partisan identity reshapes social identity. However, the effect size is small (confined to a thin tail of switchers), meaning a model that induces wholesale identity shifting would overstate the effect.

## 5. Economic Inequality Causal Over-attribution
*   **The Model Rule:** Macroeconomic inequality (e.g., Gini index, top income shares) is modeled as a direct causal driver of affective polarization.
*   **The Empirical Finding:** 
    *   While DW-NOMINATE polarization and Gini inequality are highly correlated over the 20th century, the causal arrow is circumstantial (*McCarty, Poole & Rosenthal 2006*).
    *   Cross-national trends show that other OECD countries with similar inequality trends did not experience rising affective polarization; indeed, some depolarized (*Boxell, Gentzkow & Shapiro 2024 REStat*).
*   **The Blindspot:** Treating inequality as a direct driver of agent-level affect oversimplifies US-specific institutional and racial dynamics, turning a correlational macro-statistic into an ungrounded agent-level rule.

## 6. Asymmetric Elite Dynamics
*   **The Model Rule:** Political parties polarize symmetrically in issue space.
*   **The Empirical Finding:** 
    *   Congressional polarization is highly asymmetric, with Republicans moving significantly further from the center than Democrats in DW-NOMINATE space. This is driven by self-reinforcing intraparty feedback rather than symmetric external drift (*Leonard et al. 2021 PNAS*).
*   **The Blindspot:** Modeling symmetric drift rules ignores the asymmetric feedback mechanisms unique to the party institutions.

## 7. Positional Sorting Is Imposed, Not Emergent (The "Constant Pushing" Problem)
*   **The Model Rule:** Party separation and identity alignment over 1980→2025 are produced by the `PartyPull` rule dragging agents toward party centers that are **supplied exogenously** as an empirical ANES voter-centroid time series (`data/mhv/party_centroid_series.json`, scaled by `elite_lead_factor`). Agents are Friedkin–Johnsen–anchored, so most barely move from their 1980 position.
*   **The Diagnostic Finding:**
    *   A freeze decomposition on the fitted shipped config (`docs/results/honesty_budget.json`; `scripts/audit/t35_budget_brake.py`, 6 seeds) shows that with the data-fed series frozen at its 1980 value, **party separation does not rise at all** (emergent fraction ≈ 0% / ≈100% input-carried) and **identity alignment is ≈2% emergent / 95% input-carried**. Only **affective polarization is genuinely emergent (≈87%)** — it collapses even when positional separation is held flat, consistent with the empirical fact that affect polarized far more than issue positions did.
*   **The Blindspot (as originally diagnosed):** The model **did not demonstrate that positional sorting self-organizes** — it reproduced the empirical party trajectory it was handed. Because the fed series was *voter* centroids and the measured metric is *voter* separation, the positional reproduction was close to a **conditional replay**. Two roots: (1) a deliberate S3 design choice to feed *real data* rather than hand-draw the elite trajectory; (2) a deeper tension — individual-level **stability** (FJ anchoring) structurally limits self-organized macro change, so the change had to be imposed or arrive via cohort turnover.
*   **PARTLY RESOLVED (emergence-recovery E5, 2026-06; methods §5.29; honestly reframed per the 2026-06 peer-review audit, finding F4).** Positional sorting is now **written by** an endogenous activist→elite→mass feedback loop (`abm/rules/activist_elite.py::ActivistEliteCue`): the fed `PartyCentroidSeries` and the cohort-reseed-at-centroid are both gone (`endogenous_elite=True`, `data_fed_elite=False` in the canonical `ANES_FULL_KWARGS`; the pre-E5 config is preserved as `ANES_FULL_FED_KWARGS`). Re-measured honesty budget (`honesty_budget.json`): the loop is now the **proximate writer** of the positions (loop-attributable ≈1.0 for party_sep, ≈0.96 for identity — freezing the loop collapses them to the 1980 seed), so positions are no longer a fed-series replay. **But proximate-writer ≠ emergent:** with *every* external/empirical driver frozen, the forcing-free floor is only **party_sep ≈0.28 / identity ≈0.28** — i.e. **~0.72 of each rise rides calibrated forcings** (chiefly the activist-mobilization schedule); only **affect ≈0.94** is genuinely emergent (its own mechanism). The earlier "**party_sep emergent 1.00 / ~0 input-carried**" headline conflated *loop-attributable* (proximate writer) with *forcing-free* (emergent) — that is the audit's F4. ANES scorecard **17/24** (5-seed) / realism battery **15/24** (9-seed) — **both below the project's own ≥18/24 target**; intervention buckets preserved.
*   **Residual caveats (the honest cost):** (a) the loop's **timing** is set by an exogenously-calibrated activist-mobilization schedule, not predicted — the four-cut **holdout fails the temporal cut** (`e5_holdout.md`, 1/3; the FED config passed 3/3 only by feeding the answer), and the budget shows only ~28% of party_sep is the spontaneous loop floor (~72% rides the fitted forcing). So the model **explains the mechanism but not the timing**. (b) The single-axis (`align_u`) amplification direction **over-correlates the compass axes** (corr(x,y)~0.78 vs ANES ~0.5–0.6; holdout cut-2 issue-corr slope 1.62× GSS) and runs the 2010 bucket slightly hot.
*   **The timing-exogeneity is a PROPERTY, not a to-do (the saturation-ratchet finding; methods §5.29.1).** The ~72% forcing share was pressure-tested rather than refined away. Measured offline (`scripts/audit/latent_separation.py`, [`results/latent_separation.md`](results/latent_separation.md)): the optimal re-label of the fixed 1980 seed tops out at **party_sep 0.66**, while 2025 is **1.11** — so **most of the rise sits above the absolute ceiling of any re-labeling of 1980**, broadly consistent with the ~72% forcing share. The spontaneous loop already extracts most of that 0.66 ceiling, and the ~28% forcing-free floor ≈ the latent re-sortable pool. **1980 was genuinely calm; reaching 2025 required real positional/compositional change whose pace is externally set.** A direction check confirms the latent cleavage is the population's own economic axis (PC 0.659 ≈ fed `align_u` 0.658 ≈ x-axis 0.643), not the fed answer re-entering. The affect-94%/sep-28% asymmetry is the model faithfully reproducing a real asymmetry (affect has no FJ anchor + a monotone cooling floor + self-amplification; positions are FJ-sticky). **Decision: KEEP the endogenous flip and document the finding — do not refine the loop for a bigger number.**
*   **Status: the bottom-up *mechanism* question is resolved (the loop writes the positions, not a fed replay), but the *emergence-of-the-rise* is NOT — ~72% rides calibrated forcings; the late-timing exogeneity is a documented finding (a property of mass polarization at this layer), not an open failure.** The one genuinely-open *realism* item is the axis over-correlation; endogenous party **re-sorting** is a real missing mechanism worth building **later for realism only** (FJ-compatible, faithful to the great sort) — it raises *magnitude* not *timing*, will not fix cut 1, and is hard-capped at 0.66 ≪ 1.11, so it is **not** an emergence-floor lever.
*   **R-PHASE UPDATE — emergent fraction nudged up + the cap QUANTIFIED & EXPLAINED (2026-06-18, branch `audit-surface-fixes`).** Built **R8** (`endo_mob_gain` on the loop): a party's own sorting feeds its mobilization (the polarization spiral; Mason 2018) — the *genuine* fed→earned lever, because it self-sustains when the budget freezes the fed drivers (unlike R5's media, which is itself a fed forcing → shifting sorting mob→media is forcing→forcing, *measured not to raise emergence*). On the shipped canonical (`endo_mob_gain=0.15`) the party_sep **emergent fraction rose 0.28 → 0.34** (`honesty_budget.json`, 6-seed). **The cap is now measured:** sweeping `endo_mob_gain` (`validation/audit/recal_fit.py` + `recal_budget_check.py`) shows fit-compatible emergence tops out **~0.39** — past endo≈0.15 the spiral *front-loads* polarization and breaks the per-decade §11 fit, and past endo≈2 it runs away to the ceiling (bistable positive feedback). **Why: US polarization's TIMING is exogenously event-paced (Gingrich/Fox/Trump), not a spontaneous spiral** — so the magnitude *can* be made emergent but the trajectory *shape* requires external pacing. This QUANTIFIES and EXPLAINS this blindspot: the forcing-carried share encodes real event timing; it is structural, not a fixable flaw. **Both gates now PASS** on the R-phase canonical: §11 ANES **19/24** (5-seed `phase9_anes_score`) / **18/24** (9-seed realism A2) — up from the failing 17/24 & 15/24 — with affect now in band at 2025 (−0.57) and **~83% emergent**. The pre-R-phase config is `ANES_FULL_COMMONMODE_ECON_KWARGS`.

## 8. Cultural Center-of-Mass Had No Common-Mode Channel (Cultural Position Collapsed onto Party)
*   **The Model Rule (pre-fix):** Cultural-axis dynamics were *entirely differential* — the symmetric endogenous elite loop (#7) pulls the two parties apart around a midpoint that stays ≈ 0, and `PartyPull` drags the mass to track that midpoint. There was **no mechanism anywhere** that moves the society-wide cultural *level*.
*   **The Diagnostic Finding (reality-validation workstream, 2026-06):** An independent from-scratch ANES+GSS battery (`validation/`; reproduces the ANES derivation exactly, so the anchor is trustworthy) found the partisan cultural **center of mass** sat **~0.10–0.20 too progressive in the mid-period** (worst ~1996–2000), while real voters were culturally *traditional* then (ANES partisan cult +0.10→+0.22; GSS public traditionalism z +0.2) and liberalized only in the 2010s. The endpoint matched (so the §11 band gates were blind — they don't pin the center; the pytest suite didn't move at all on the fix). Visible symptom: the Republican cloud's lower-left tail spilling into the progressive-redistributive quadrant (R-in-LL @2000 ≈ 15% vs ANES 8%).
*   **The Blindspot:** Because cultural position was a pure function of party, the engine could **sort** the parties but could not represent **secular cultural liberalization** — empirically ~69% cohort replacement / ~31% within-cohort period drift (raw-GSS APC decomposition; Firebaugh/Baunach/Brooks-Bolzendahl). An experiment steepening the (already correctly-signed) cohort gradient moved the center by *nothing*: party pull washed out the generational signal at the historical turnover rate.
*   **RESOLVED (2026-06; methods §5.30).** A **common-mode cultural channel** (`abm/rules/cultural_common_mode.py`, `cultural_common_mode=True` in canonical `ANES_FULL_COMMONMODE_KWARGS`): agents carry a `birth_year` with the *measured* ANES birth-cohort cultural gradient; the society-wide common mode m(t) = mean baseline declines **emergently** as traditional cohorts turn over (turnover 0.007/tick). Only demographic primitives are fed (gradient + turnover); the trajectory emerges. New battery 6 FAIL → 3 FAIL (cultural center fixed; sorting preserved/improved); scorecard neutral (17/24, the 1-cell delta is the orthogonal affect issue, #1); phase10 buckets unchanged. Pre-fix config preserved as `ANES_FULL_ENDOGENOUS_KWARGS`.
*   **Residual caveat (honest):** the fix tracks the **robust GSS** cultural level (+0.07 at 1996), so the battery's three remaining HIGHs (it targets the ANES partisan center, which carries a mid-90s "+0.22 hump" GSS does not corroborate) are *declined*, not chased — pushing the common mode higher would over-fit a non-robust ANES item-composition artifact. The within-cohort period component (~31%) is implicitly folded into the calibrated turnover rate rather than modeled as a separate channel.
*   **Honesty-budget exclusion + magnitude provenance (added per the 2026-06 peer-review audit, P4 / F7, F12).** This channel is a **rigid, sorting-invariant level shift** of the issue vector, so it does **not** enter any differential metric (`party_sep`, `affect`, `identity_alignment`) and is therefore **excluded from the honesty budget** (`honesty_budget.json` tracks only differential metrics). The aggregate trajectory "emerges" from a *fed* gradient + a *fed* turnover rate, but the **level result itself is ~100%-fed / ~0%-emergent** in the budget's sense — so the budget's emergent-fraction headline **omits this directly-fed result**. The "~69% cohort replacement" weight licensing the channel is **N, not L**: it is the model's own non-identified APC self-computation (`validation/gss_cohort.py` OLS omits age, collinear with `year−birth`), where cited papers report ≈33% (Baunach 2012) to ≈55% (Brooks-Bolzendahl 2004). The *direction/mechanism* is L; the *magnitude* is N (methods §5.30).

## 9. Economic Center-of-Mass Had No Common-Mode Channel (Economic Position Collapsed onto Party)
*   **The Model Rule (pre-fix):** The *same* architecture gap as #8, on the **economic** axis. Econ-axis dynamics were entirely differential (symmetric elite loop #7 + `PartyPull`), so the partisan economic **center of mass** was pinned ≈ 0 the whole arc. No mechanism moved the society-wide economic *level*.
*   **The Diagnostic Finding (reality-validation workstream, 2026-06):** After #8 was fixed, the same from-scratch battery showed the partisan econ center stuck ≈ 0 while ANES rises to ~+0.15 (rightward) in the mid-90s and declines to ~−0.05 by 2024 (**non-monotone**). Decomposition of the 1996 Republican-econ residual (−0.19): **center-of-mass LEVEL error −0.16 (≈84%)**, party half-GAP error −0.04 (≈16%). The visible symptom is the same Republican lower-left (progressive-redistributive) tail as #8 — the econ half of it.
*   **The Blindspot:** Because econ position was a pure function of party, the engine could not represent the secular **economic policy mood** (Reagan→Clinton "end of big government" rightward into the mid-90s; post-2008 thermostatic leftward reaction). Unlike #8 this tide is **non-monotone**, so the monotone demographic primitive that fixes the cultural axis **cannot** generate it — the mechanism is the thermostatic policy mood (Erikson–MacKuen–Stimson, *The Macro Polity*; Wlezien), which is **exogenous** to party sorting.
*   **RESOLVED — but via a FED forcing, not emergent (2026-06; methods §5.31).** An **economic common-mode channel** (`abm/rules/cultural_common_mode.py::CommonModeEconomic`, `economic_common_mode=True` in canonical `ANES_FULL_COMMONMODE_ECON_KWARGS`, amplitude 0.09) snaps the econ common mode to an exogenous policy-mood offset curve each tick (rigid, sorting-invariant). Gate ON cuts the econ-COM error 64% (mean|err| 0.084→0.030), improves the Republican wrong-quadrant tail every measured year, leaves the cultural axis bit-identical, and preserves sorting. Gate OFF = bit-identical to head (canonical-arc SHA-256 unchanged). Pre-fix config preserved as `ANES_FULL_COMMONMODE_KWARGS`.
*   **Honesty caveat (this is the weaker claim — read it):** this channel is a **fed forcing**, not emergent like #8. Provenance (corrected per the 2026-06 peer-review audit, P4 / F7): the channel as shipped is **N** (a fed, hand-specified forcing curve) + **E** (amplitude/shape extrapolated); only the abstract *idea* of a thermostatic common mode is L (Erikson–MacKuen–Stimson) — it is **not** an L-supported emergent mechanism. The driver is a parsimonious event-anchored curve whose single free scalar is the amplitude; the real **Stimson Annual Policy Mood** (downloaded from stimson.web.unc.edu) was used to *corroborate* its direction, **not replayed** — fed literally it injects a spurious +0.20 Tea-Party-era 2012 econ spike (government-spending mood ≠ economic self-placement; falsified in `validation/exp_econ_commonmode.py`). The target arc is **triply confirmed** (ANES + GSS helppoor+eqwlth `mid-90s +0.106 → late −0.057` + Stimson), so matching it is not ANES over-fitting — but the engine does **not** derive the mood itself, it is told it. Amplitude 0.09 matches the robust GSS level; the noisier ANES mid-90s +0.15 hump is *declined*, not chased.
*   **~0%-emergent / ~100%-fed; excluded from the honesty budget (added per the 2026-06 peer-review audit, P4 / F7).** `CommonModeEconomic` snaps the econ common mode to its target each tick (`relax = 1.0`): output ≈ target to ~3 dp, so #9's "64% error cut" is **mechanical** (a consequence of feeding the right level), not emergent. Being a rigid, sorting-invariant level shift, it does not enter any differential metric and is **excluded from the honesty budget** (`honesty_budget.json` `_provenance`: "this budget does not track" the econ COM). The budget's emergent-fraction headline therefore **omits the model's single most directly-fed result** — read the two together.

## 10. Cultural Sorting Is Mis-Paced (Front-Loaded; Misses the 2012–2020 Acceleration)
*   **The diagnostic finding (analysis workstream, 2026-06; full study + figures in [`docs/results/cultural_sorting_analysis.md`](results/cultural_sorting_analysis.md)).** With the common-mode *levels* fixed (#8/#9), a from-scratch root-cause of the partisan **separation (gap)** curve — extracted year-by-year from raw ANES, GSS-confirmed — found the residual divergence is **entirely on the cultural axis, and it is a TIMING error, not a level or amplitude error**. The real econ and cultural gaps sort on **different schedules**: economics is steepest ≈2008, culture is **back-loaded** (steepest ≈2024, the big jump 2012→2020). The model's single smooth mobilization schedule cannot be low-early *and* spike-late on culture while pacing economics correctly, so it **front-loads** culture.
*   **The two divergences (model − real, clean canonical econ-ON, seeds 0–2):**

    | axis | early 86–92 | mid 94–04 | late 08–24 |
    |---|---|---|---|
    | ECON | +0.013 | −0.009 | −0.005 (within 1–2σ — **fine**) |
    | CULT | **+0.113** | +0.038 | +0.030 |
    | SEP | +0.074 | +0.018 | +0.017 |

    (a) **Early cultural OVER-separation (1986–90, +0.11 to +0.16):** real cult gap 0.12/0.17, model 0.25/0.34 — the model starts the parties far too culturally divided (contradicts both ANES and GSS). (b) **2020-peak UNDERSHOOT:** real cult gap **0.783 @2020** vs model **~0.675** (−0.108); real sep peaks 1.147 vs model 1.010 — the model misses the sharp 2016→2020 cultural spike-then-revert.
*   **Not a calibration-only or overfitting problem.** Econ is fine (within 1–2σ). The real curve is a smooth logistic S (**R²≈0.95**) **+ modest event-locked structure** (residuals ~1.5–2σ; cultural near-monotone) — **not** idiosyncratic / overfitting territory, so it *is* in principle matchable. Figures: `validation/figures/real_sorting_curves.png`, `validation/figures/model_vs_real_sorting.png`.
*   **Presentation caveat (separate from model dynamics).** The **shipped web export + display-EMA run cooler** than the clean engine, so part of the *perceived* under-separation is a publish-path artifact: econ gap @1996 **0.307** (export) vs 0.413 (clean) vs 0.445 (real); sep @2020 **0.972** (export) vs 1.010 (clean) vs 1.147 (real). A cheaper, non-mechanism lever on the visual complaint is the export/repack path.
*   **Cause of the real accelerant — no consensus single cause (see analysis doc §5).** The 2012+ cultural acceleration ("Great Awokening" / "Racing Apart") has a consensus *contributing set* — Obama-era racialization (Tesler), the 2014 Ferguson/BLM catalyst, the 2011–12 media/social-media salience inflection (Goldberg), amplified by social sorting (Mason), predating Trump — with two **unresolved debates**: causal **direction** (Tesler race→party vs Engelhardt *BJPS* 2021 party→race) and a **measurement-artifact** challenge (Engelhardt *AJPS* 2021, observational equivalence). So the magnitude target itself is partly contested.
*   **Honest fix direction (HYBRID, not pure emergence; not yet built).** The elite/activist cue-taking mechanism is **endogenous in the engine** (the activist→elite→mass loop), but its **trigger/timing is exogenous** (outside the model boundary). Honest path: an **exogenous, documented driver timed independently of the ANES cultural series** (Ferguson-2014 step / Goldberg race-salience series / the existing `data_fed_media` curve) → fed through the **existing cultural cue-loop** → cultural sort **emerges**, one fitted amplitude, matching a **robust core not the full magnitude**, **paired with lowering the early cultural differential**. Provenance **L+E+N**. Timing it by the observed cultural gap would be the fed-answer trap ("(B) in an (A) costume"). Falsify-first prototype: `validation/exp_cultural_hybrid.py`.
*   **Falsify-first result (2026-06): the cue-route hybrid is FALSIFIED on H1.** An event-timed cultural cue (early dampener + Ferguson-2014-anchored 2012–2020 release) routed through the existing `party_cue`→PartyPull channel is **harmless** (H3 pass: econ untouched, no party_sep overshoot, common-mode center preserved) and **reduces the early over-separation ~40%** (H2 partial), but **cannot reproduce the 2012–2020 surge** (H1 fail: 2020 cult gap stays 0.67–0.73 vs real 0.783; the model still peaks 2016). Root cause: the activist→elite→mass loop amplifies along a **fixed 2D direction**, so cultural timing is structurally **bound to economic timing**; a cue schedule shifts the cultural *level* but can't *re-time* it. A real fix needs **structural decoupling** of the cultural axis in the loop (axis-specific mobilization/direction, or a second cultural loop) — not a hand-drawn cue. See `docs/results/cultural_sorting_analysis.md` §7; figure `validation/figures/cultural_hybrid_falsified.png`.
*   **⏸ NEXT STEP / PLANNED FIX (PARKED — structural, not a forcing).** The only honest fix is an **architecture change**, not another exogenous forcing: give the activist→elite→mass loop (`abm/rules/activist_elite.py`) an **axis-specific amplification direction** — decouple the currently *fixed* econ:cultural ratio (the single `party_axis[pid]` direction) — **or add a second, cultural-only loop**. Then an **event-timed cultural driver** (Ferguson-2014-anchored / Great-Awokening salience, timed by documented events — see the HYBRID bullet above) can **re-time the cultural axis independently of economics** instead of being welded to it. This is queued as the explicit next step. Rationale: the cheap **cue-schedule hybrid was already falsified** (commit `ee10451`) precisely *because* the single fixed-direction loop binds cultural timing to econ timing — so no forcing routed through that loop can work; the decoupling must happen in the loop's structure. Scope note: this is a larger change than the common-mode channels (#8/#9) and carries its own honesty profile (the decoupling is a *mechanism*; the cultural trigger/timing remains an exogenous documented driver), so it warrants its own spec + measure-then-bless cycle.

## 11. No Endogenous Depolarization — the (Net) One-Way Ratchet
*   **The diagnostic finding (2026-06 peer-review audit, finding F5; verified by a 135-tick census).** The canonical shipped baseline **cannot endogenously depolarize**. Every live rule in the build is either **divergent / sorting** (`PartyPull`, the activist→elite→mass loop, `EliteDrift`, `IdentitySorting`/`MeasuredAlignment`, `BacklashRepulsion`, `TieRewiring`, `MediatedAnimus`) or an **off-axis level shift** (the common-mode channels #8/#9). The one rule that could pull positions *together* — `BoundedConfidenceInfluence` — is ~inert in the arc (turning it off changes 2025 `party_sep` by only ~+0.014; see #BC / P7) **and** it only converges agents *within* a camp (homophilous neighbours), not across the party line, so it tightens clusters rather than depolarizing the society. The out-party-affect **warming** path is effectively **dead code**: no cooperative/cross-party edges are seeded, so the cooperative-mute warming branch never fires (0 endogenous warming ticks over 135 ticks); affect is monotone-cooling with a floor. The only forces that *reduce* polarization in the model are the **X5/X6 imposed resets** (exogenous interventions), never the baseline dynamics.
*   **Important nuance (audit correction — it is "net one-way," not a literal pure ratchet).** The Friedkin–Johnsen **anchor pull** toward each agent's fixed 1980 position **is** a working restoring force (`FJ_ALPHA * stubbornness`, ~10–20% per-tick damping toward the anchor), so positions are not free to run away unbounded and a *frozen-forcing* run relaxes partway back. The honest statement is therefore: **the baseline has no endogenous force that moves the society from a sorted state back toward a less-sorted one** (depolarization in the macro sense), even though individual-level anchoring resists further divergence. "Net one-way ratchet," not "pure ratchet."
*   **Why it matters / falsifiability.** This is a **first-class, falsifiable structural assumption**, not a bug: it asserts that — within this model's boundary — depolarization is something that must be *done to* the system (an intervention), never something the coupled channels produce on their own. It sits in tension with the model's own blindspot **#5** (cross-national cases where societies *did* depolarize) and with any claim that the engine could forecast a future cooling. Cross-reference: audit finding **F5**; the BC inertness is **P7** / methods §5.10-BC.
*   **RESOLVED on the AFFECT axis and on the POSITION axis as CAPACITY (2026-06-18, R-phase; branch `audit-surface-fixes`).** The R-phase added the missing restoring channels, all gated and isolation-tested, validated by a **generic regime battery** (G1; `validation/audit/layer1_battery.py`) that judges the mechanism layer across forcing×restoring regimes on *directional* criteria, **not** US bands. Result: the coupled mechanism layer now **polarizes, rests, AND reverses** as conditions dictate — the literal ratchet is gone:
    *   **Position** — the warming/restoring forces now have real traction: **R2** cross-pressure damping retargeted to `PartyPull` (the actual `party_sep` driver), **R3** cross-cutting bridge tie formation, **R6** two-signed thermostatic feedback on the separation overshoot.
