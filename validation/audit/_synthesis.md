# AUDIT SYNTHESIS

## Executive summary
polarlab is a competently engineered, unusually self-aware agent-based model whose CODE largely does what its docstrings say — the delta pipeline, FJ damping, RNG-stream integrity, and most mechanism implementations are sound. The problems are not bugs; they are (1) a circular calibrate-and-score architecture, (2) a flagship output (affective polarization) that is materially out of band, and (3) a top-level honesty/status layer that systematically overstates what the lower-level docs honestly concede.

Three things every reviewer should hold onto. FIRST, the "fit" is largely fed, not earned. The headline metric, party_sep, has ~72% of its 1980→2025 rise supplied by an activist-mobilization schedule whose convexity is ABC-fit directly to the ANES party_sep curve it claims to explain; only ~28% is the loop's own spontaneous floor (confirmed against the live honesty_budget.json, not the docs). The model's own four-cut holdout fails the temporal cut on exactly this metric. SECOND, the scoring is circular: a single ANES recode (respondent_coordinates.csv) sets the knobs AND defines the bands AND builds the Wasserstein targets, so the §11 "15–18/24 cells in band" and "w2≈0.92" are goodness-of-fit, not validation — the band scorecard cannot falsify the model. THIRD, the flagship affect output is out of the grounded ANES thermometer band at EVERY decade (too cold, growing to ~3x band halfwidth by 2025; raw partisan mean runs to −1.27, off the [−1,1] scale, masked by a clip-on-read), and the canonical build ships with affect saturation turned OFF.

Layered on top is a pattern of stale-favorable headlines that invert the honest numbers buried two docs deeper: CLAUDE.md advertises "scorecard 18/24, realism 18/24" and "party_sep ~1.00 emergent / ~0 input-carried"; the real measurements are 15/24 (a FAIL of the project's own ≥18/24 gate, unenforced by any test) and 0.28 emergent / 0.72 fed. model_blindspots #7 ("RESOLVED... emergent 1.00") is contradicted by the very JSON file it cites. The two center-of-mass "fixes" (blindspot #8/#9) are ~100%-fed rigid snaps that are deliberately excluded from the honesty budget, so the budget's emergent-fraction headline reads as completeness while omitting the most directly-fed results.

The encouraging counterweight: the literature SPINE is genuinely solid (Pettigrew-Tropp, Mason, ANES thermometer, DW-NOMINATE all check out and the highest-stakes extrapolation is honestly E-tagged), the engine core is correct, and almost every weakness IS documented somewhere in the repo. This is a disclosure-and-framing failure at the headline layer, not a hidden-corruption failure.

## Overall verdict
As a peer reviewer: the MODEL is mechanically sound and the ENGINEERING is strong, but the central scientific claim — that US positional polarization "emerges" from the model's dynamics — is not earned by the evidence presented, and the headline framing overclaims relative to the author's own honest measurements. I would not reject it, but I would require major revisions to the claims (not the code) before it could be presented as anything more than a calibrated illustration.

Is it sound? The delta pipeline, FJ damping, and rule implementations are correct (verified across the code-correctness and mechanism streams). What is NOT sound is the inferential leap from "the model reproduces the trajectory" to "the model explains/generates the trajectory."

What does it actually establish vs overclaim? It establishes a MECHANISM (an activist→elite→mass amplification loop can convert a small latent cleavage into large party separation) and that affective polarization is the one genuinely emergent quantity (~94%, because affect has no anchor and a monotone cooling floor). It does NOT establish the TIMING or MAGNITUDE of US polarization: ~72% of the party_sep rise rides a forcing fit to the answer, and the timing fails out-of-sample (the holdout temporal cut). The "positional sorting now emerges / blindspot #7 RESOLVED" headline is the overclaim — the honest statement is "mechanism endogenous, timing/magnitude calibrated."

Is it "just a sorting model"? Substantially, yes — and this is the most important structural finding. The shipped baseline is a one-way ratchet: every live rule is divergent/sorting or off-axis, there is exactly one (inert) endogenous convergence force, out-party affect can only cool endogenously (the warming path is dead code absent an intervention), and depolarization exists only as externally-imposed resets (X5/X6). Classic structural drivers (geography→position feedback, material conditions, candidate agency, the asymmetric-Republican-shift signature) are absent or routed into sorting-invariant level shifts that by construction cannot move the polarization metrics. So it adjudicates "how fast an assumed monotone sort proceeds," not "what drives polarization vs depolarization." That is a defensible scope for a 1980→2025-US teaching artefact (the US did monotonically polarize), but it must be stated as a top-level falsifiable assumption, not left implicit in which knobs are zero.

Is the fit earned or fed? Fed, on the quantities that matter most. party_sep: 72% fed via an ANES-shape-fit schedule. Econ + cultural center-of-mass: ~100% fed by rigid per-tick snaps, and excluded from the honesty ledger. The genuinely EARNED, independent checks (held-out GSS slope, Pew overlap, the affect trajectory's emergent shape) are the credible part — and the affect one is out of band. The §11 scorecard cannot earn anything because it is scored against the same recode that set the knobs.

Bottom line: a well-built, honestly-instrumented sorting engine whose lower-level documentation is admirably candid, wrapped in a headline/status layer that overstates emergence, fidelity, and resolution. The fixes are almost entirely re-labeling and re-blessing, not re-engineering — which is both the good news (cheap to fix) and the indictment (the honest numbers already exist in-repo and were simply not propagated to the surfaces readers see first).
